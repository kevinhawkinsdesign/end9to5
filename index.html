Building your business while employed is the most challenging period. The work is hard, and it feels lonely in the challenges you're facing. You don't know what to expect, and you may find it hard to ask for advice.

Despite the early difficulties, building your own business is the best thing you can do. It transforms your life with confidence and empowerment. It brings financial freedom. It gives you a joyful, successful venture of your own design.

Our mission with End9to5 is to give you a clear view of what the employed-to-founder transition holds. From deciding to leave to building a thriving practice, and all the challenges in between — we want you to feel prepared, comfortable, and calm as you face your future.

Questions:

Do I need a fully formed idea to start? +
No. The first phase is entirely about exploring, testing, and refining ideas. Many start with a direction, not a fully formed business. We'll help you validate and sharpen it through customer research and market testing.
What if my idea fails during the program? +
Failure is baked in as learning. You'll ship something, get real feedback, iterate, and learn. Most "failures" are just data that help you adjust course. Many participants pivot their idea mid-program based on what they learn—that's success.
How much time does this take per week? +
10-15 hours per week. Audio modules during commutes, worksheets on weekends, one monthly in-person workshop (2-3 hours). Designed for people still employed, building on the side. The audio format means you control when and where you engage.
Is this different from other programs? +
Yes. Most programs focus on strategy or mindset—we focus on your ability to actually follow through. Alongside the roadmap, you build the internal system needed to handle uncertainty, take action, and stay consistent. It's designed specifically for the employee-to-founder transition.
What industries do you work with? +
Services, consulting, coaching, training, digital products, tech-enabled solutions, events, wellness, and food/beverage concepts. We avoid hardware, manufacturing, defense, and team sports. If you're unsure, apply—we'll let you know if it's a fit.
Do I need startup capital or initial revenue? +
No. We work with people at zero revenue and minimal startup capital. The program is designed around validation and lean launch. You'll learn how to validate with customers before spending money.
What's included in the 1:1 coaching options? +
Your base price includes 2 check-in calls (one free in month 1, one at your choosing). Optional tiered upgrades give you additional 1:1 access to Kevin (branding, positioning, go-to-market strategy) or Ora (inner development, identity work, confidence). Most people find the cohort + group sessions + 2 check-ins sufficient.
How does the cohort work if I join late? +
Cohorts are 6 months rolling, starting monthly. You join at any point and work through modules at your own pace via audio + worksheets. Monthly in-person workshops are repeating sessions, so if you miss one, you'll see it again. You complete modules as they cycle through—flexible entry means you're never "behind."
What if I'm a business partner with someone else? +
Great—we love co-founders. Both of you should sign up, but enter the same business name. We'll recognize you're a partnership and bill you as one participant (one fee, shared access). You'll both be in the cohort together.
Is there legal or financial advice included? +
We don't provide legal or financial advice directly—that's outside our scope. But we have a vetted referral list of English-speaking lawyers, accountants, and gestors in Barcelona. We also offer an AI-assisted business registration tool to guide you through Spanish paperwork in multiple languages.
Will I be pushed toward venture funding or investor pitch training? +
No. We're not an accelerator with a fund to fill. This is about building the business that fits your life—not chasing a funding round. If you want to raise later, you'll have a much stronger foundation from this program, but it's entirely your choice.
What happens if I can't make the monthly in-person workshops? +
Life happens. The core of the program—audio modules, worksheets, templates—is all async and self-paced. In-person workshops are for community and real-time interaction, but they repeat monthly. If you miss one, you'll catch the next cycle. Optional 1:1 sessions can be scheduled around your availability.
How do you handle the technical side of building? +
We focus on strategy, positioning, and launching with what you have. If you need to build a website, app, or SaaS product, we can recommend vetted developers or no-code tools. We teach you how to communicate your needs clearly so you can work with builders efficiently.
What formats can I submit homework in? +
Whatever works for you. Voice notes, written documents, sketches, photos, Google Docs, spreadsheets—we accept all formats. We use AI to convert everything into consistent feedback. The goal is to meet you where you are, not force you into one way of working.
Can I do this entirely online? +
Almost. The monthly in-person workshops in Barcelona are optional for remote participants, but we recommend attending at least a few to build real connections and feel the cohort energy. Everything else—audio, worksheets, coaching—is available online from anywhere.
What's the refund policy? +
We offer a full refund within 14 days of your first check-in call if you're not satisfied. After that, we work with you on next steps—no refunds, but we can discuss options for pausing or adjusting your participation.
Do you offer payment plans or financing? +
Yes. You can pay upfront, or use Klarna and SeQura for flexible monthly installments. We work with third-party providers so you manage payments directly with them—we receive the full amount upfront, and you're not at risk of us chasing you for late payments.
What makes the inner development coaching different? +
Ora's coaching pillar addresses the emotional and identity barriers that stop most people from making the leap. Fear of the unknown, impostor syndrome, identity shifts—these are real obstacles. We treat them as seriously as business strategy. This is rarely covered in other programs, and it's often the difference between sustaining your venture and getting stuck.
How will I know if this is right for me? +
You're a good fit if: you're employed right now, you have an idea or area of expertise you want to build around, you're willing to do the work, you're open to inner development, and you genuinely want to create something (not just chase quick money). The free check-in call is designed for you to decide if it aligns with where you are.
